Stat Society Game Night

Problem 1

When I go fishing, I catch one fish per hour on average. (Assume they arrive independently, w/constant probability per unit time interval.)

Bet on the chance that I catch at least one fish in the next hour. (Odds: 3:1)

The result is:

I caught a fish!
I didn't catch a fish

Problem 2

We will flip cards (out of a 52 card deck) 2 at a time. If the cards are both red, we give them to you. If the cards are both black, I keep the cards flipped. Otherwise, we set them aside. When no cards are remaining, if you have more cards than me, I will give you 2 times however much you pay me. Otherwise, you win nothing.

You can either get 1 token or play this game.

The result is:

Cards are Tied. (You Lose)

Problem 3

A bag has 10 Blue Balls and X Red Balls, where X is a number chosen uniformly from 1 to 10. We draw out balls 2 at a time without replacement. If they are the same color, we add a blue ball to the bag. If they are different colors, we add a red ball to the bag. We repeat this process until one ball remains in the bag.

Bet on whether the remaining ball is red. (2:1 odds)

The result is:

The Last Ball is Red
The Last Ball is Blue

Problem 4

You're sending job applications to 5 firms: Morgan Stanley, Lehman Brothers, UBS, Goldman Sachs, and Merill Lynch. You have 5 envelopes on the table neatly typed with names and addresses of people at these 5 firms. You also have carefully written out 5 cover letters, personalized to each of the firms. Unfortunately, your best friend has randomly stuffed each cover letter into each of the envelopes for you and sent them out, ignoring the fact that the letters are personalized!

You're now worried all 5 of them are sent to the wrong firms. Bet on the probability of this event. (1:1 Odds)

The result is:

All 5 Letters are in the Wrong Envelopes. Whoops.
At least 1 is in the right envelope! Yay!

Problem 5

You have a room of 100 mice, 50 male and 50 female. Assume that in each mating season, each pair of male and female mice will mate, produce 2 children, and then die. Assume that all the mice who do not mate also die. 40 of your male mice are normal - when they mate with a female mouse, they will produce a boy and a girl. The remaining 10 mice possess a segregration-distorter gene on their Y chromosome that causes them to only father male mice. (This gene is hereditable, with 100% probability.) No female mice have the gene. Assume that male mice with the segregation-distorter gene mate have exactly the same chance as mating with a female mice as a male mouse without. After two generations, we select a mouse at random.

Bet on the probability the mouse chosen has the segregation-distorer gene. (3:1 Odds)

The result is:

The mouse has the gene.
The mouse does not have the gene.

Problem 6

You are given 100 coins. Among them, 1 coin has heads on both sides, and the other 99 are fair coins with heads and tails. You select a coin at random and flip it 8 times without checking if it has two heads. All 8 times, the coin lands on heads.

Bet on the probability that the coin is a fair coin (1:2)

The result is:

Fair Coin
2 Headed Coin
